How to Build a Habit Tracker Database in Notion

Step 1: The Daily Log Database

Create a new database called "Daily Habits." Each entry represents one day. Here are the properties:

PropertyTypeWhat It Does
DateTitle (or Date)Today's date. Format: 2026-03-25. Use as title for easy browsing.
ExerciseCheckboxDid you exercise today? Yes/no.
ReadingCheckboxDid you read for 20+ minutes?
Deep WorkNumberHours of focused work. Not a checkbox — you want to see the actual time.
JournalingCheckboxDid you write in your journal?
No AlcoholCheckboxA "negative habit" — tracking what you didn't do.
ScoreFormulaAuto-calculates: how many checkboxes were checked out of total.
Important: Start with 3-5 habits maximum. Not 12. Not "everything I want to improve." 3-5 habits you can actually do every day. You can always add more later.

Step 2: The Score Formula

This formula gives you a daily completion percentage. Assuming 4 checkbox habits:

round((toNumber(prop("Exercise")) + toNumber(prop("Reading")) + toNumber(prop("Journaling")) + toNumber(prop("No Alcohol"))) / 4 * 100)

This gives you a number like 75 (meaning 3 out of 4 habits completed). It's more motivating than seeing individual checkboxes because it turns your day into a single score.

Step 3: The Streak Counting Trick

True streak counting in Notion requires complex rollups or external tools. But here's a simpler approach that works 90% as well:

  1. Create a "This Week" filtered view — filter Date to "is within the past 7 days"
  2. Look at the Score column — if every day shows 75%+, your streak is alive
  3. For a specific habit streak, sort by Date descending and scan the checkboxes visually — the moment you see an unchecked box, that's where your streak broke

It's not as automated as an app that shows "🔥 23 day streak!" — but honestly, checking a visual streak once a week is more meaningful than a gamified counter you stop noticing after day 5.

Step 4: The Weekly Completion View

This is the view that keeps you honest. Create a new Table view with:

  • Filter: Date is within "Past 7 days"
  • Sort: Date descending
  • Visible properties: Date, all habit checkboxes, Score

Every Sunday, open this view. Count how many days scored 75%+. That's your weekly completion rate. Write it down somewhere (or add a "Weekly Review" database that tracks it).

Aim for 80% weekly completion, not 100%. Perfection isn't the goal — consistency is. If you hit 5 out of 7 days, you're building real habits.

Common Habit Tracking Mistakes

1. Tracking Too Many Habits

The #1 reason habit trackers get abandoned. You start with 10 habits, miss a few days, feel guilty, and stop opening the tracker entirely. Start with 3. Seriously. Add one more only after you've maintained 3 for a full month.

2. All-or-Nothing Thinking

You miss one day and think "well, the streak is broken, might as well skip the rest of the week." This is the most destructive pattern in habit building. A single missed day is noise. Two missed weeks is a trend. Never miss twice in a row.

3. Habits That Are Too Vague

Don't track "be healthier." Track "walk 30 minutes" or "eat a vegetable at lunch." Vague habits can't be checked off, which means you never get the satisfaction of completion. Make every habit a clear yes/no.

4. Not Reviewing

A habit tracker without weekly reviews is just a checkbox graveyard. The power isn't in checking boxes — it's in seeing patterns. Which habits consistently get done? Which ones don't? Why? The review is where behavior change actually happens.

5. Overcomplicating the System

If your habit tracker has rollups, relations to 3 other databases, conditional formatting formulas, and a dashboard with charts — you've built a system that's more fun to build than to use. Keep it simple. Checkboxes and a score formula. That's it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I track habits on mobile with Notion?

Yes. The Notion mobile app works for checking boxes, though it's slower than desktop. Pro tip: add a "Daily Habits" shortcut to your phone's home screen using Notion's mobile widget. One tap to open, check your boxes, done.

How do I handle weekday-only habits?

Add a "Day Type" select property (Workday / Weekend). Create separate filtered views for workday habits and weekend habits. Don't try to track gym attendance on days you've designated as rest days — it artificially lowers your score.

What about time-based habits like "meditate 10 minutes"?

Use a Number property instead of a Checkbox. Track minutes. Then create a formula: if(prop("Meditation") >= 10, true, false) to convert it to a yes/no for your score calculation. You get both the specific data and the simple checkbox experience.

Is there a way to get streak notifications?

Not natively in Notion. But you don't need them. The weekly review habit is more powerful than daily notifications. Notifications create external motivation (which fades). Reviews create internal awareness (which lasts).
